<p><p>The sterility indicators market comprises two main types: chemical indicators and biological indicators. Chemical indicators are substances that change color or form in response to specific sterilization conditions, providing immediate feedback on the process. Biological indicators, on the other hand, use live microorganisms to verify the effectiveness of sterilization; they require culture growth to confirm sterility. Both types are crucial in ensuring the safety and efficacy of medical devices and equipment in healthcare settings.</p></p>
