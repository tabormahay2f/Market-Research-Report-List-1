<p><p>The Wireless Power Controller ICs market comprises two main types: Transmitter ICs and Receiver ICs. Transmitter ICs are responsible for generating and transmitting power wirelessly to devices, often utilizing resonant inductive coupling or magnetic resonance. Receiver ICs, on the other hand, are designed to harvest the energy transmitted by the transmitter and convert it into usable power for the receiving device. This market segment is driven by the increasing demand for efficient, cable-free charging solutions across various applications, including consumer electronics and electric vehicles.</p></p>
